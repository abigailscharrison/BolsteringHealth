North Carolina Nonprofit

Connecting North Carolinians with the care and resources they need to thrive.

Bolstering Health is dedicated to providing holistic services to members of the community, in an effort to restore families that have been damaged through substance abuse or untreated mental health disorders. True wellness comes from stability, education, and the ability to sustain yourself. We pair care with teaching, mentoring, and accountability so people build long-term sustainability and everyday stability — including staying consistent with their appointments.

Mission Snapshot

We strengthen communities by pairing care with capability — connecting people to housing, recovery, mental health support, healthy living, and financial literacy, then staying with them until stability sticks.

Our Approach

Meet people where they are. Remove barriers. Follow through. We blend warm outreach with practical coordination so care is accessible, consistent, and sustainable.

Family Restoration Focus

We provide holistic, community-based services that help restore families impacted by substance use or untreated mental health disorders. In partnership with The Carter Clinic, PA, we connect clients to counseling, medication management, primary care, and peer support — promoting healing and helping restore the family unit across underserved North Carolina communities.

Housing as Health

Housing is healthcare. We secure housing and teach the systems to sustain it.

Bridge to Independence (18–24)

For youth aging out of foster care

Transitional housing with step-down supports, paid apprenticeships, coaching, and mental health linkage.

Stability After Incarceration

For returning citizens

Rapid safe housing, paid transitional work, skills training, and ongoing case management.

Families & Individuals in Need

For households facing housing instability

Placement and stabilization with budgeting, benefits navigation, and linkages to Steward & Seed and Community Health & Wellness.

National Affordable Housing Gap

A new report by the National Low Income Housing Coalition (NLIHC) finds a national shortage of 7.1 million affordable and available rental homes for extremely low-income renter households — those earning at or below the poverty line or 30% of their area median income. This means there are only 35 affordable homes for every 100 extremely low-income renters nationwide. The Gap: A Shortage of Affordable Homes is released annually and analyzes affordability and availability across every state and major metro area.

Our Team & Approach

Bolstering Health’s care management team has years of experience with substance-using populations. We inspire clients to engage in treatment or recommit after setbacks, using last-known contacts and verified databases to re-connect, educate, and collaboratively solve barriers that prevent care.

We recognize that most people who disengage have relapsed and face elevated risks — overdose, re-imprisonment, and other harms of chronic substance dependence. We intervene early with compassionate accountability and consistent follow-up.

Financial literacy + behavior + professionalism — with an investing mindset: every purchase and decision should be an investment toward financial freedom and a thriving life.

Financial Literacy & Empowerment

Budgeting, saving, credit management, and planning — applied to real life.

Behavioral Health & Wellness

Workforce Development & Reentry

Professionalism, communication, and consistency for job readiness and advancement.

Program Phases

  1. Understanding the System — Foundations: budgeting, saving, credit, psychology of money.
  2. Building Financial Systems — Budgets, goals, planning, and investing basics.
  3. Professionalism & Communication — Self-presentation and advocacy for workplace success.
  4. Growth & Accountability — Ongoing check-ins, progress logs, and habit tracking.
